Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, extreme temperature changes, and a cratered, rocky surface. Despite its proximity to the Sun, it's not the hottest planet. A day on Mercury lasts longer than its year due to its slow rotation.

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere rich in carbon dioxide. Its surface is extremely hot due to the greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet. Venus rotates slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets, and it's often called Earth's twin.

Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has liquid water, a breathable atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems. Earth's surface is dynamic with continents, oceans, and weather systems. The planet orbits the Sun once a year and rotates every 24 hours, giving us day and night cycles.

Mars, the red planet, is known for its iron-rich soil and dry, desert-like surface. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system. Mars has seasons, polar ice caps, and signs of ancient water flow, sparking interest in its potential to support past or future life.
Jupiter is the largest planet, a gas giant with a powerful magnetic field and dozens of moons. It's famous for the Great Red Spot, a giant storm. Jupiter's atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with colorful cloud bands and extreme weather. Its immense gravity influences many objects in space.
Saturn is best known for its stunning ring system, composed of ice and rock particles. It's a gas giant like Jupiter, with a low density and strong winds. Saturn has over 100 moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere. The planet's beauty and complexity continue to fascinate astronomers.
Uranus is an ice giant with a pale blue color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, possibly due to a past collision, leading to unusual seasons. The planet has faint rings and over two dozen moons. It's cold, distant, and mysterious in many ways.
Neptune is the farthest known planet, dark blue due to atmospheric methane. It's an ice giant with supersonic winds and violent storms. Neptune has faint rings and many moons, including Triton, which orbits backward. Though distant, it's a dynamic world full of energy and motion beneath its icy clouds.
